A note of importance: All numbers have to be integers or fractions. ℜ represents any valid number.
Input
Another note of importance: if ƒ is any expression and ℕ is any positive integer, then:
Exponentiation is limited to ℜ^ℕ and (ƒ)^ℕ.
However, you can do ℜ1^(ℕ, ℜ2) which means ℜ1...×ℜ2, ℕ times (ℜ1×ℜ2×ℜ2×ℜ2...)
Output
Yet another note of importance: if ⊜ is an operator, then:
⊞⊜⊕ = taking the ⊜⊞ of ⊞, ⊕ times (i.e. the hyperoperation after ⊜)
⊞1⊜{⊕⊞2} = taking the ⊜⊞2 of ⊞1, ⊕ times
