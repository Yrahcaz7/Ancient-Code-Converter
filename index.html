A note of importance: All numbers have to be integers or fractions. ℜ represents any valid number.
Input
Notes If ƒ is any expression and ℕ is any positive integer, then:
Exponentiation is limited to ℜ^ℕ and (ƒ)^ℕ.
However, you can do ℜ1^(ℕ, ℜ2) which means ℜ1...×ℜ2, ℕ times (ℜ1×ℜ2×ℜ2×ℜ2...)
Output
Notes If ⊜ is an operator, then:
⊞⊜⊕ is taking the ⊜⊞ of ⊞, ⊕ times (i.e. the hyperoperation after ⊜)
⊞1⊜{⊕⊞2} is taking the ⊜⊞2 of ⊞1, ⊕ times
