Another note of importance: if ƒ is an expression and ℤn is any integer, then:
Exponentiation is limited to ℤ1^ℤ2 and (ƒ)^ℤ.
However, you can do ℤ1^(ℤ2, ℤ3) which means ℤ1×ℤ2, ℤ3 times (ℤ1×ℤ2×ℤ2×ℤ2...)

Yet another note of importance: if ⊜ is an operator, then:
⊞⊜⊕ = taking the ⊜⊞ of ⊞, ⊕ times (i.e. the hyperoperation after ⊜)
⊞1⊜{⊕⊞2} = taking the ⊜⊞2 of ⊞1, ⊕ times
